What is an Estuary

An estuary is place where freshwater from rivers and lakes and saltwater from the ocean meet, Estuaries may seem useless but they are important

Estuaries help the Economy

The estuary also helps the Puerto rican economy, as of 2019 11 million tourists visit estuaries every year.

Protects from Floods and Storms

The estuary helps with floods and it can help prevent tropical storms by soaking up most of the water and a lot of its pollutants.

Important Locations

Some of the most important national parks and government buildings are located on Estuaries land, if we dont preserve the estuaries the buildings might collapse

Vast Food Supply

Estuaries are home to many animals and it also helps us by providing food. As of 2019 we catch over 350,000 pounds of fish in the San Juan estuary alone.

Why Should we make sure there is good quality water in Estuarios?

if the water quality is not within the ranges of the PH and the DO then millions of animals would suffer and die from poor water quality. Not only would aquatic animals suffer but so would plants that supply themselves with water. There are also industries that revolve completely around clean water and with the water quality like this they would plummet.

  • There are 308 species of plant that heavily rely on the water quality and the estuary
  • 160 different types of birds 124 different types of fish and 19 different types of reptiles

Where is the water quality the lowest? And where is the Highest?

Estuario goes to 26 different bodies of water. In these areas the water quality is Mildly Alkaline, two bodies of water are strongly alkaline and the other 2 are neutral

  • Neutral: Quebrada Blasina, Laguna Torrecillas 3
  • Strong Alkaline: Laguna los Corozos, Laguna San José 2.

How do different Organizations like Estuari Test the Water? And what do they Measure?

Every week PEBJS(Programa del Estuario en la bahía de San Juan) personnel collect water from different bodies of water throughout the San Juan area. The analysis then takes place in laboratories. The scientist measures the PH, Legend Ph(measures alkaline), dissolved oxygen etc.

  • If the PH is anywhere between 6.5 and 8.5 then the water quality is good.
  • DO(Dissolved Oxygen) if the DO is above 6.5-8 then it is good quality

How to make a difference

The quality of the water can only be tested in laboratories which a lot of people do not have. However, just because you dont have a laboratory does not mean you cant make a difference for the water quality.

Recycling

Recycling keeps plastics and other materials that are contaminated and polluted out of the water in estuaries.

Household chemicals in the Trash

Household chemicals can have a huge impact on the water quality. It's ok to use them but don't put them down the drain

Organic Foods

Eating organic food may not seem to make an impact however, to grow some food it takes transportation and a lot of negatively impacting chemicals

Supporting Charities

Supporting charities is the easiest way to make an impact either by donating or volunteering and that small effort will make an impact.

Fat and Grease does not go down the drain

After cooking do not put any fat or grease down the drain as it can clog sewer pipes which leaks into local bodies of water.

Watching for oil leaks

Oil leaks are dangerous for different bodies of water. You can make a difference by just checking your car for any traces of leaking oil.
